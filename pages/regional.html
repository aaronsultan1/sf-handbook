MMA operates across five regions, each with different Salesforce workflows, primary objects, and key contacts. This guide helps you understand how your region uses Salesforce — and how to collaborate with other regions.

US / Global Team

America/New_York timezone • Manages the core MMA membership base, all NA/Global campaigns, Pardot email lists, and lead conversion automation

Key People

Aaron Sultan — Sales & Membership Enablement Manager; Salesforce admin, leads automation, CRM data quality
Amanda Hyland — AVP Brand Membership; member relationship management
Greg Stuart — CEO; global/NA membership conversations at executive level
Shauna Peck — Membership Project Coordinator
Erin Disney, Denise Champion, Alec Foster — US team members
Jason Chase, Kevin Granath — US team

Typical Workflow

  • 1Lead arrives from form or event → enters SF as Lead
  • 2Lead is automatically matched and converted to a Contact on the correct Account
  • 3Contact added to Pardot list for member communications
  • 4Campaign created for upcoming event → Contacts invited
  • 5Post-event: attendance logged as Campaign Member statuses
  • 6Renewal season: Account renewal dates flagged → Account team reaches out

Primary Objects Used

  • Accounts — track membership status, renewal dates
  • Contacts — main list management and outreach
  • Leads — intake from web forms; do not touch manually
  • Campaigns — all events/webinars with naming convention

Pardot Usage

  • US/Global team is the primary Pardot user
  • Pardot lists built from Salesforce Contact segments (Active, NA/Global, mailable)
  • Email opt-outs sync between Pardot and SF automatically
  • Campaign data in SF feeds Pardot engagement tracking

Notes & Gotchas

  • Default reports filter Region IN ('NA','Global') — adjust when needed
  • The UNKNWN and Admin accounts are US-team managed — do not edit them
  • Board member exclusions (Is_Board_Member__c) are most relevant to US outreach
  • Aaron Sultan is the go-to for all SF data questions and lead conversion

Singapore, Indonesia, India, Vietnam, Sri Lanka • Heavy Opportunity usage for Local Council renewals and event sponsorships

Key People

Shanti Tolani — Country Head (Indonesia)
Rudrashish Nag — Sri Lanka
Prerana Shukla — Manager Marketing & Events (India)
Que My Vo — Head of Partnership (Vietnam)

Typical Workflow

  • 1Renewal season begins → Rohit's team reaches out to member and manages the conversation
  • 2Finance / Membership create the Opportunity in Salesforce (APAC teams do not create Opportunities directly)
  • 3As deal progresses, communicate updates to Finance/Membership so stages are kept current
  • 4Invoice sent by Finance → Opportunity moves to Invoice Sent (Renewal Notification)
  • 5Payment received → Finance marks Opportunity as Closed Won; Account Status updates
  • 6Create Campaign in SF for APAC events (Smarties, IMPACT, Innovate) — APAC teams do enter Campaigns

Key Events & Campaigns

  • Indonesia: Smarties Unplugged
  • India: IMPACT events
  • Vietnam: MMA Innovate
  • APAC-specific summits and roundtables

APAC-Specific SF Fields

  • APAC_Membership_Type__c — type of APAC membership (Local Council, etc.)
  • APAC_Sponsorships__c — APAC sponsorship details on Opportunity
  • Status__c: Active-Local Council is common for APAC members
  • Membership_Type__c: Local Council or Regional

Notes

  • APAC data is filtered separately from NA/Global reports — they have their own list views
  • Rohit Dadwal is the first point of contact for APAC SF questions
  • Opportunity naming: "2026 APAC Local Council Renewal_CompanyName" is the standard

For regional mailings: When sending communications to a regional board audience, use the country-level field for that region rather than Is_Board_Member__c, which only covers the global boards. For example, to invite Turkey BOD members to a Turkey-specific event: filter on Current_BOD_Turkey_member__c = true.

Regional Preference Center Fields

Each region has its own Preference Center where contacts can manage what types of MMA communications they receive. These preferences sync from Pardot back to Salesforce. There are three preference categories per region.

Region Programs & Events Research & Insights Smarties Awards
NA / Global Preference_Ctr_Programs_NA__c Preference_Ctr_Research_NA__c Preference_Ctr_Smarties_NA__c
EMEA Preference_Ctr_Programs_EMEA__c Preference_Ctr_Research_EMEA__c Preference_Ctr_Smarties_EMEA__c
APAC Preference_Ctr_Programs_APAC__c Preference_Ctr_Research_APAC__c Preference_Ctr_Smarties_APAC__c
LATAM Preference_Ctr_Programs_LATAM__c Preference_Ctr_Research_LATAM__c Preference_Ctr_Smarties_LATAM__c

When building a regional audience for a Pardot email, always filter on the relevant Preference Center field to respect opt-in choices. For example, to send EMEA event invitations: include contacts where Preference_Ctr_Programs_EMEA__c = true (or not opted out, depending on your org's field implementation). Bülent manages the EMEA Preference Center in Salesforce; Aaron Sultan manages NA/Global.

Cross-Region Collaboration

💡
Global campaigns: For global events like POSSIBLE that span regions, the campaign's Region__c is set to Global and the name prefix is ALL. Each region may also create their own sub-campaigns if needed for regional tracking.
⚠️
Don't filter out regional data unintentionally. If you're working on a global analysis and filtering on Region__c, make sure to include all relevant regions. The US team's default filter of Region IN ('NA','Global') will miss APAC, EMEA, and LATAM data.
