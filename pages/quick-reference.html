How Do I...

🔍

Find if a company is a member

  • 1Search for the company name in the Salesforce global search bar
  • 2Open the Account record
  • 3Look at the Status__c field — any value starting with "Active" = member
  • 4Check Next_Renewal_Date__c to see when they renew
👤

Find all contacts at a company

  • 1Search for the company name in global search
  • 2Open the Account record
  • 3Scroll down to the "Contacts" related list
  • 4All contacts at that company are listed there
📣

Create a new campaign for an event

  • 1Go to Campaigns tab → click "New"
  • 2Name: follow REGION_TYPECODE_YYYYMMDD_EventName
  • 3Set Type (Webinar, Forum/Event, etc.) and Status (Planning or Active)
  • 4Fill in Start/End Date, Region__c, Event_Name__c
  • 5If it's a session under a larger event, set the ParentId
  • 6Save — the campaign is ready for members to be added
➕

Add someone to a campaign

  • 1Open the Campaign record
  • 2Scroll to "Campaign Members" related list
  • 3Click "Add Members" and search by name or email
  • 4Set Status: Registered (pre-event) or Attended (post-event)
  • 5Click Add / Save
🔄

Move a contact to a different account

  • 1Open the Contact record
  • 2Click Edit (or the pencil icon next to Account Name)
  • 3Search for and select the correct Account
  • 4Save — the contact now inherits the new account's membership status
  • 5Verify Account_Status__c and Is_Member__c updated correctly
💼

Create an Opportunity for a renewal

  • 1Navigate to the Account record for the member company
  • 2Scroll to "Opportunities" related list → click "New Opportunity"
  • 3Name: "2026 [Region] Membership Renewal_CompanyName"
  • 4Type: Membership - Renewal; Stage: Prospecting
  • 5Set Close Date (expected renewal date) and Amount
  • 6Advance stages as the deal progresses
🏗️

Build a mailable contact list (US/Global)

  • 1Go to Reports → Create a Contacts report
  • 2Filter: Account.Status__c LIKE 'Active%'
  • 3Filter: Account.Region__c IN ('NA', 'Global')
  • 4Filter: HasOptedOutOfEmail = false
  • 5Filter: IsEmailBounced = false
  • 6Filter: Email != null
  • 7Filter: Is_Board_Member__c = false AND Current_B2B_CMO_Council_member__c = false
🆕

Add a new contact to Salesforce

  • 1First, search for them — they may already exist
  • 2Go to Contacts tab → click "New"
  • 3Fill in: First Name, Last Name, Email, Title
  • 4Account Name: link to their company Account (required)
  • 5If their company isn't in SF, create the Account first
  • 6Review the duplicate warning before saving

Status Values at a Glance

Account Status (Status__c)

  • Active-Parent Main membership holder
  • Active-Subsidiary Covered under parent
  • Active-Subsidiary (irrelevant) Covered, low engagement
  • Active-Local Council APAC local member
  • Pending Deal in progress
  • Inactive/Cancelled Former member
  • Non-Member No membership

Campaign Status

  • Planning Being set up — not yet live
  • Active Open for registrations
  • Completed Event is over
  • Aborted Cancelled

Lead Status

  • New Just arrived, not reviewed
  • Working Being reviewed
  • Qualified Confirmed good lead
  • Unqualified Not a fit (spam, student, etc.)
  • Converted Now a Contact — done

Opportunity Stage (simplified)

  • Prospecting Identified, not yet contacted
  • Qualified Interest confirmed
  • Negotiation/Review Terms being discussed
  • Commitments Verbal/written agreement
  • Invoice Sent Awaiting payment
  • Closed Won Payment received!
  • Closed Lost Deal fell through

Campaign Member Status — Engaged

  • Attended Confirmed attendee
  • Checked In Checked in at event
  • Paid-Attended Paid and attended
  • Watched / On-Demand Watched recording
  • Downloaded Downloaded content
  • Responded Completed survey

Campaign Member Status — Not Engaged

  • Registered Signed up, attendance unknown
  • Invited Outreach sent only
  • Sent Email sent to them
  • No Show Registered, didn't attend
  • Cancel Cancelled their registration
  • Declined Said no

Campaign Naming Cheat Sheet

Format

REGION _ TYPECODE _ YYYYMMDD _ EventName
Token Options
REGION ALL (global)  |  NAM (North America)  |  APAC  |  EMEA  |  LATAM
TYPECODE Wbnr (Webinar)  |  Wbs (Workshop/Session)  |  Eml (Email)  |  Tsh (Thought Leadership)
YYYYMMDD Date of the event. Example: 20260429 = April 29, 2026
EventName CamelCase, no spaces. Keep concise. For sub-events, put parent name first: POSSIBLE2026_SessionName

Examples

ALL_Wbnr_20260408_TheStateOfMarketingAnalytics
APAC_Wbs_20260408_MMAIndonesiaDataInsights
NAM_Eml_20260408_SMARTIES2026
ALL_Wbs_20260429_POSSIBLE2026_MFCandB2BCouncilLunch
EMEA_Wbs_20260915_DMEXCO2026_Roundtable

Dos and Don'ts

✅ Do This

  • Follow the campaign naming convention every time
  • Check Account Status__c (not contact) to verify membership
  • Use MKTG_Country__c for country filtering on Accounts
  • Filter for Region__c IN ('NA','Global') for US reports
  • Exclude board members and opted-out contacts from mass outreach
  • Create a new Opportunity for each year's renewal
  • Keep the Next_Steps__c field updated on Opportunities
  • Search for a person before creating a new Contact (check for duplicates)
  • Contact Aaron Sultan if you have questions about leads or data quality

🚫 Don't Do This

  • Don't manually convert leads — the daily auto-script handles it
  • Don't edit or move contacts on the UNKNWN account manually
  • Don't use Country__c for country filtering — use MKTG_Country__c
  • Don't create a campaign without following the naming convention
  • Don't email contacts where HasOptedOutOfEmail = true
  • Don't create a Contact without linking it to an Account
  • Don't amend a prior year's Closed Won Opportunity — create a new one
  • Don't assume a contact is a member — always check the Account record
  • Don't forget the B2B CMO Council field — it's not in Is_Board_Member__c
