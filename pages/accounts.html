Accounts (Companies)

An Account represents a company. Every Contact, Opportunity, and Campaign interaction eventually ties back to an Account. Understanding Account structure is foundational to using Salesforce at MMA.

What Is an Account?

In Salesforce, an Account is a company — a brand, agency, tech platform, media company, or any organization that has a relationship with MMA. Every person (Contact) must belong to an Account. Every membership renewal (Opportunity) is tied to an Account. If you want to know whether a company is a member, you look at their Account record.

MMA has thousands of Accounts ranging from active global member corporations to non-member companies that have simply attended an event or expressed interest in the past.

⚠️
Membership lives on the Account, not the Contact. When you want to know if someone is a member, look at their Account's Status__c field — not the Contact directly. The Contact's membership-related fields are just mirrors of the Account.

Membership Status

The most important field on an Account is Status__c. This is the primary membership filter used across all MMA reports, scripts, and data exports.

💡
The golden rule: To find all members, filter Status__c LIKE 'Active%'. Any Status__c value beginning with "Active" means the company is a current member.

Status__c Values Explained

Active-Parent
The main (parent) membership. This company holds the primary membership record. Most global and national members fall here.
Active-Subsidiary
A subsidiary or child company covered under a parent company's membership. They get member benefits through the parent.
Active-Subsidiary (irrelevant)
Covered under a parent, but classified as "irrelevant" — typically too small or inactive to be meaningfully engaged. Still technically active.
Active-Local Council
Member of a regional Local Council (common in APAC). These accounts are active but tracked separately from global memberships.
Pending
Membership is in process — a deal is likely in the Opportunity pipeline, but not yet closed/paid.
Inactive/Cancelled
Was once a member but their membership has lapsed or was cancelled. Important to track for win-back efforts.
Non-Member
Has never been a member, or the account was created for tracking purposes only. No active membership relationship.

Account Hierarchy: Parent & Child Accounts

Large companies often have multiple Salesforce records — a parent company and one or more subsidiary accounts. This is called an Account Hierarchy.

Example: A Large Holding Company

Imagine a major holding company like a global advertising group. In Salesforce it might look like:

  • Parent Account: "GlobalAdGroup Inc." — Status: Active-Parent — holds the membership
  • Child Account A: "GlobalAdGroup Agency NYC" — Status: Active-Subsidiary — covered under parent
  • Child Account B: "GlobalAdGroup Media Buying" — Status: Active-Subsidiary (irrelevant) — covered but not engaged

Contacts at any of these three accounts have member access, but the primary membership relationship is managed at the parent level.

💡
Tip: When looking at a contact's membership, always check if their account is a subsidiary. If so, navigate up to the parent account to see the full membership details, renewal date, and dues.

Ecosystem (Industry Type)

The Primary_Ecosystem__c field on Account classifies what type of company a member is within the marketing and media industry. It drives segmentation for outreach, reporting, and membership composition analysis. As of 2026, MMA has 1,016 active member accounts across all ecosystems.

🧭
The most important segmentation in Salesforce: Every active member account is either a Marketer or a Solution Provider. That two-way split is the foundation for almost all audience segmentation at MMA — used in reports, mailable counts, outreach campaigns, and board/council targeting. Everything else flows from understanding this distinction.

The Core Divide: Marketer vs. Solution Provider

Single ecosystem value

Marketer

Any organization with an internal marketing team that invests in advertising, data, and media to build its own brand, grow its customer base, or drive demand for its own offerings. The defining characteristic is that they are buyers in the marketing ecosystem — they spend on media, data, and technology to reach their own audiences, rather than selling those capabilities to others.

This spans a wide range of organizations: consumer brands, retailers, financial services, healthcare systems, pharma, automotive, hospitality, entertainment, sports, and B2B technology companies. What unites them is the presence of an internal marketing function with its own advertising budget — not the type of product or service they offer.

Ford, Coca-Cola, Bank of America, Marriott, Walmart, American Express, GEICO, Eli Lilly, L'Oreal, Unilever, Mastercard, The Home Depot, Heineken, Verizon, Mayo Clinic, Canva, NBA Properties, Workday, JP Morgan Chase, Paramount

297 active accounts
Every other ecosystem value

Solution Provider

Any company that makes its business by selling into the marketing ecosystem — offering services, technology, media inventory, data, or expertise to organizations that do their own marketing. The defining characteristic is that they are sellers or enablers in the ecosystem, not buyers of advertising for their own brand.

Solution Provider is an umbrella that covers a wide range of business models: agencies that plan and execute on behalf of clients, media platforms and publishers that sell ad inventory, ad tech and mar tech companies that power the infrastructure, data and measurement firms, and research or trade organizations. What unites them is that their value proposition is directed at marketers, not at end consumers or general audiences.

📣 Agency — media, creative, PR, research agencies
📺 Seller — platforms, publishers, media that sell ad space
⚙️ Enabler — ad tech, mar tech, data, measurement tools
📡 Carrier/Operator — telecom/mobile network operators
🔬 Associate — research institutes, trade orgs, consultancies
526 active accounts
🔑
The quick test: Ask — "Does this organization have an internal marketing team that invests in advertising, media, or data to build its own brand and reach its own audiences?" If yes → Marketer. If the company makes its business by selling advertising capabilities, media inventory, marketing technology, agency services, or data to other organizations → Solution Provider. When in doubt: are they spending marketing budgets, or selling to the people who do?

Ecosystem Values — Definitions & Examples

Marketer
Any organization with an internal marketing team that invests in advertising, media, or data to build its own brand and reach its own audiences. Spans consumer brands, retailers, financial services, healthcare, pharma, auto, hospitality, entertainment, sports, and B2B technology. The largest single ecosystem in MMA's membership.
Agency
Companies that manage marketing on behalf of brands. Includes media agencies (GroupM, Publicis, IPG Mediabrands, Omnicom), creative agencies, PR firms (Weber Shandwick), research/measurement firms, and strategy consultancies that run marketing practices. They plan, buy, and execute on behalf of clients.
Seller
Companies that own and sell media inventory or ad space. Includes social/digital platforms (Google, Meta, TikTok, LinkedIn, Pinterest, Snap, Reddit), streaming services (Netflix, Hulu, Spotify, Disney+), traditional broadcasters (ESPN, NBCUniversal), and retail media networks (Amazon Ads, Walmart Connect, CVS Media Exchange). They monetize audiences.
Enabler
Technology and data companies that power the marketing infrastructure. Includes ad tech platforms (The Trade Desk, AppsFlyer), measurement & verification (DoubleVerify, Integral Ad Science, Nielsen), identity/data (LiveRamp, TransUnion, Amperity), and marketing software (Adobe). They build the tools marketers and sellers use.
Carrier/Operator
Telecom network operators whose infrastructure underpins mobile marketing. Currently one active member globally: MTN (EMEA). Historically more relevant when SMS and carrier-based mobile was a primary channel.
Associate
Non-commercial affiliated members — research institutes, trade associations, academic-adjacent organizations. Examples: The Advertising Research Foundation (ARF), Marketing Science Institute, Analytic Partners. Not typical commercial members but formally affiliated with MMA.
Academic Institution
Universities and research institutions with an academic membership type. Rare in active count — most appear in specific regional markets.
Holding (for holding accounts only)
Reserved exclusively for Holding Pattern and similar internal-use accounts. Should never appear on a real company record. If you see this on a normal Account, flag it.
Start Up
Early-stage companies in the marketing/media/technology space, typically in a startup membership tier. Rare in the current active member count.

Edge Cases & Nuances

The ecosystem field was assigned manually by people who understood each company's primary role. A few patterns worth knowing:

Same company, different ecosystem depending on the entity

Walmart (the retailer) = Marketer — they advertise to consumers to drive store traffic. Walmart Connect = Seller — they sell ad inventory to brands on the Walmart platform. Two separate Account records, two different ecosystems, both correct.

Google (global) = Seller. Google India Private Limited = Enabler. Different legal entities, different primary functions in that market.

AT&T and Verizon = Marketer — even though they're telecoms, they're members because of their marketing function (spending billions on advertising), not their carrier function.

Kantar appears as both Agency and Enabler

Different Kantar entities do different things. Kantar Argentina, Kantar Vietnam = Agency (they provide research services to brands). Kantar (global), Kantar Germany = Enabler (they provide measurement and data products). When you see Kantar, check the specific Account record.

SOQL segmentation shorthand

Marketer only:

Primary_Ecosystem__c = 'Marketer'

Solution Provider (all non-Marketer, excluding Academic and Holding):

Primary_Ecosystem__c IN ('Agency', 'Seller', 'Enabler', 'Carrier/Operator', 'Associate', 'Start Up')

Key Roles — Leadership Lookups

MMA tracks named executives at each member account using dedicated Key Role fields on the Account object. Each field is a lookup that points directly to a Contact record — verified and maintained by Shauna. These fields are the authoritative way to identify CMOs, CEOs, and other C-suite contacts at member companies.

💡
Use Key Roles, not Title text, for members. Filtering by Contact.Title LIKE '%CMO%' is unreliable — titles vary wildly in free-text. For active member accounts, Account.CMO__c is the verified, Shauna-maintained source of truth. Title-based filtering is the fallback for non-member/prospect accounts where Key Roles may not be populated.

Special Accounts to Know

🚫
UNKNWN Account — This is a system placeholder. Leads and contacts that can't be matched to a real company are temporarily placed here. Do NOT manually move contacts to or from this account. This queue is managed automatically in the background. Talk to Aaron Sultan if you see a contact here that should be matched to a real account.
⚠️
Admin Holding Account — An internal bucket for contacts that failed matching during lead conversion. Similar to UNKNWN — contacts here are awaiting cleanup and re-assignment to their correct company account. Do not use this account for real data entry.
🚫
Holding for Non-Accounts — A legacy workaround account that should no longer be used. Before MMA had a dedicated Salesforce admin, when a contact changed jobs and their new company didn't exist in Salesforce yet, they were placed here rather than creating a proper Account record. That practice is now obsolete. If you see contacts in this account, do not move them without flagging to Aaron Sultan first — they need to be matched to a real Account or have one created. Never add new contacts here.
👔
Holding Pattern — An active, intentional account for contacts who are between jobs. When a known contact (particularly a senior marketer or executive MMA tracks) leaves their company but hasn't yet announced a new role, they're moved here rather than deleted. This preserves their engagement history and keeps them visible so MMA can re-engage quickly when they land somewhere new. Contacts in Holding Pattern are there on purpose — do not remove or re-assign them without checking with Aaron Sultan. When the person joins a new company, move them to that company's Account.

How to Find Member Accounts

When building a list view or report of member companies, always filter on Status__c:

SOQL Filter for Active Members

WHERE Status__c LIKE 'Active%'

This catches all four Active status values: Active-Parent, Active-Subsidiary, Active-Subsidiary (irrelevant), and Active-Local Council.

SOQL Filter for NA/Global Members Only

WHERE Status__c LIKE 'Active%' AND Region__c IN ('NA', 'Global')

This is the standard scope for US-team reports and mass outreach lists.

Dues Tier Structure

Member dues are stored directly in Salesforce and are structured by ecosystem and revenue band. Each account is assigned a dues tier based on what kind of company they are and how large they are — the tier then determines their annual membership fee.

FieldAPI NameNotes
2026 Dues Charged X2026_Dues_Charged__c Actual dues billed for the current membership year. Prefer this over Current_dues__c for 2026 reporting.
Dues Ecosystem Tier Ecosystem_Dues_Tier_2013_new__c The formal dues category. Values reflect the metric used for each ecosystem: Brand Marketer – Total Advertising Spend, Agency – Gross Income, Media Seller – Total Mobile Ad Revenues, Technology Enabler – Total Mobile Revenues.
Dues Tier — Marketers Dues_Ecosystem_Marketers__c Revenue band for Marketer accounts: Marketers 1 ($0–25M), Marketers 2 ($25–100M), Marketers 3 ($100–500M), Marketers 4 ($500M–2B), Marketers 5 ($2B+). Based on total advertising spend.
Dues Tier — Sellers Dues_Ecosystem_Sellers__c Revenue band for Media Seller accounts, based on total mobile ad revenues.
Dues Tier — Enablers Dues_Ecosystem_Enablers__c Revenue band for Technology Enabler accounts, based on total mobile revenues.
Payment Frequency Payment_Frequency__c How the account pays dues. Value of 1 = annual; 2 = installments.
