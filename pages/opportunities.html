Opportunities (Deals & Renewals)

Opportunities track every membership renewal, sponsorship deal, and potential new member. Regional teams — especially APAC, EMEA, and LATAM — depend heavily on Opportunities to manage their pipelines and revenue.

What Is an Opportunity?

An Opportunity in Salesforce represents a potential or confirmed deal between MMA and a company. At MMA, the three main types of deals tracked as Opportunities are:

Membership Renewal

When an existing member renews their annual membership. This is the most common type — every member account generates a renewal Opportunity each year.

Sponsorship

A company sponsoring an MMA event, conference, or program. Common in APAC, EMEA, and LATAM — often large multi-thousand dollar deals.

New Membership

A prospective company that's never been a member before is considering joining MMA. Less common, but tracked through the same pipeline.

The Opportunity Pipeline (Stages)

Every Opportunity moves through a series of stages from initial interest to payment received. This is the pipeline that sales and membership teams use to track deal progress.

There is also an Invoice Sent (Renewal Notification) stage — used specifically for membership renewals when the renewal invoice is sent but payment hasn't been received yet. And Closed Lost is used when a deal falls through.

Stage Descriptions

StageMeaningWho Acts Next
Prospecting Initial identification — we think this company might renew or be a sponsor, but no conversation has happened yet MMA rep reaches out
Qualified Confirmed that this deal is real and worth pursuing — the company has expressed interest MMA rep qualifies the deal details
Negotiation/Review Both sides are discussing terms, pricing, and scope Back-and-forth negotiation
Commitments: Verbal/Written The company has agreed to the deal verbally or in writing — not yet formal contract Prepare contract/term sheet
Term Sheet Sent A term sheet has been sent to the company for review Company reviews and signs
Contract Sent Full contract has been sent Company reviews and countersigns
Invoice Sent Invoice has been sent; awaiting payment Company processes payment
Invoice Sent (Renewal Notification) Renewal-specific: renewal notice/invoice sent, payment pending Company processes payment
Closed Won (Payment Received) Payment received — the deal is complete. Account Status should update to Active. Update Account, log the win
Closed Lost Deal fell through — company didn't renew or declined the sponsorship Log reason, consider follow-up strategy

Naming Conventions for Opportunities

Good naming makes it easy to find opportunities in reports and list views. Use this pattern:

Format

{Year} {Region} {Type}_{CompanyName}

Examples

2026 APAC Local Council Renewal_PT Telkom Indonesia
2026 EMEA Sponsorship_DMEXCO_CompanyName
2026 NA Membership Renewal_CompanyName
2026 LATAM Sponsorship_MercadoLibre

Who Uses Opportunities by Region

APAC — Rohit Dadwal & Team (Singapore, Indonesia, India, Vietnam, Sri Lanka)

APAC uses Opportunities very heavily — it's the primary tool for managing the Local Council membership renewals and event sponsorships across the region.

Typical APAC workflow:

  • Create Opportunity at Prospecting stage when renewal season begins
  • Advance through stages as conversations happen
  • Send renewal invoice → move to Invoice Sent (Renewal Notification)
  • Payment received → move to Closed Won
  • Update Account's Status__c to Active if needed

Key examples: Indonesia Smarties Unplugged, India IMPACT events, Vietnam Innovate event sponsorships

EMEA / MEA / EU — Melis Ertem, Elvin Altun, Bülent Doruker (CRM Director)

EMEA uses Opportunities for membership renewals and event sponsorships across Turkey, Germany, EU, and MEA markets. Bülent Doruker in Istanbul manages CRM automation for the region.

Typical EMEA workflow:

  • Create renewal or sponsorship Opportunity
  • Manage through negotiation stages
  • Contract and invoice sent
  • Close as Won or Lost with notes

Key examples: DMEXCO sponsorships, Germany CMO Lunches, Turkey Strategic Memberships

LATAM — Fabiano Destri Lobo & Thais Schauff

LATAM manages large sponsorship deals — often the highest-value individual Opportunities in the entire MMA pipeline. These deals can range from tens of thousands to over $170,000.

Key examples: Mercado Libre ($170k), ICBC Argentina, Play Digital/MODO

  • Primarily sponsorship Opportunities for regional events
  • Follow the same pipeline stages as other regions
  • Often have longer negotiation cycles due to deal size

US / Global — Greg Stuart, Aaron Sultan & US Team

The US team has lighter Opportunity usage compared to regional teams. Their primary use is logging Closed Won membership deals after the fact, and tracking Greg Stuart's global/NA membership conversations.

  • Log Closed Won for new US/Global member deals
  • Track large global renewal conversations
  • Generally, US renewals are more automated and tracked at the Account level
💡
When to create an Opportunity vs. just updating the Account: Create an Opportunity whenever there's an active deal to track — a renewal conversation, a negotiation, or a sponsorship discussion. If a renewal just auto-renews with no active conversation, you may just update the Account fields directly. When in doubt, create the Opportunity — it provides a historical record.

Opportunity Naming for Renewals: Year Matters

Each year's renewal should be a new Opportunity record. Don't edit the prior year's closed Opportunity. Create a new one for the current year. This preserves the deal history and makes year-over-year reporting accurate.

⚠️
Tip: When a prior year's Opportunity is Closed Won, clone it (use the Clone button in Salesforce) to create the new year's renewal Opportunity. Update the name, close date, and amount as needed. This saves time and ensures all fields carry over correctly.
