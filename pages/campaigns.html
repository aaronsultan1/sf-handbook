Campaigns (Events & Webinars)

Every MMA event, webinar, email blast, and content piece should have a Campaign record in Salesforce. Campaigns track who engaged, who attended, and how they found out about us. The naming convention is critical — automation depends on it.

What Is a Campaign?

In Salesforce, a Campaign is a record that represents any organized MMA activity — an event, a webinar, an email send, a survey, or a content download. Campaigns are how MMA tracks engagement: who registered, who attended, who downloaded something, who was just invited.

Every time someone interacts with a Campaign (registers for a webinar, attends an event, downloads a report), they become a Campaign Member — a link between that person (Contact or Lead) and that Campaign.

⚠️
The naming convention is not optional. MMA's automation scripts that sync campaign data to Pardot, Outreach, and internal reports parse campaign names to identify region, type, and date. An incorrectly named campaign can break reporting or fail to sync. Always follow the REGION_TYPECODE_YYYYMMDD_EventName format.

Campaign Hierarchy (Parent & Child Campaigns)

Large events like POSSIBLE often have a parent campaign record and many child campaign records for individual sessions. This hierarchy allows MMA to track both overall event attendance and session-level engagement.

Example: POSSIBLE 2026

Parent Campaign: POSSIBLE2026_registration — tracks all registrants

  • Child Campaign 1: ALL_Wbs_20260429_POSSIBLE2026_MFCandB2BCouncilLunch
  • Child Campaign 2: ALL_Wbs_20260429_POSSIBLE2026_KeynoteSpeaker
  • Child Campaign 3: ALL_Wbs_20260429_POSSIBLE2026_WorkshopAI

Each child campaign has its own Campaign Members list. The parent campaign's member count rolls up from all children.

Campaign Member Statuses

When a Contact or Lead is added to a Campaign, they become a Campaign Member with a Status. The status tells you what their relationship to the campaign is — did they attend? Just register? Were they invited but didn't show?

💡
Status matters for reporting. When generating attendance reports, filter Campaign Members by statuses in the "Confirmed Engagement" group. Statuses like Invited and Sent indicate outreach was done, not that someone attended.

How to Add Someone to a Campaign

1
Open the Campaign record

Navigate to the Campaigns tab and find the event you want to add someone to. You can search by the campaign name.

2
Scroll to the Campaign Members related list

On the campaign record page, scroll down to find the "Campaign Members" related list. You may need to expand it.

3
Click "Add Members"

Click the "Add Members" button. A search window will open. Search by name or email to find the Contact (or Lead) you want to add.

4
Set the Status

Select the appropriate Campaign Member Status. For pre-event additions: use Registered. For post-event additions: use Attended, Checked In, or the relevant status.

5
Save

Click Add/Save. The person is now a Campaign Member. They will appear in campaign reports and Pardot can use this data for segmentation.

💡
Adding many people at once: For large events with 50+ attendees, use the "Import" feature from the Campaign Members related list, or coordinate with Aaron Sultan to bulk-import via a data upload. Manual one-by-one additions work fine for small lists (under 20 people).
