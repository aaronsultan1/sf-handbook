Leads (Inbound Inquiries)

Leads are people who have submitted a web form or been added to Salesforce but haven't yet been matched to a company. MMA has an automated daily conversion process — understanding how it works will save you from creating data problems.

What Is a Lead?

In Salesforce, a Lead is an unconfirmed person — someone who has shown interest in MMA but hasn't been verified and matched to a company Account yet. Leads are a temporary state. The end goal is always to convert a lead into a Contact linked to the correct Account.

Think of leads as the "waiting room" of Salesforce. When someone fills out a form on MMAGlobal.com, registers for a webinar, or is imported from an event list, they typically start as a Lead. Once Salesforce (or the automation script) identifies their company, the lead is promoted to a full Contact.

Where Do Leads Come From?

Leads arrive in Salesforce through several channels, tracked in the LeadSource field:

Lead SourceHow It Gets Into SFCommon Examples
Web Forms Salesforce Web-to-Lead forms on MMAGlobal.com push directly to SF POSSIBLE conference registrations, "Contact Us" submissions, research download forms
Webinar Registrations Webinar platform (Zoom, GoTo) registrations synced to SF campaigns; new registrants become leads MMA webinar sign-ups from non-members or new contacts
Event Sign-Ups Event registration lists imported after events Attendees from POSSIBLE, Smarties, or other conferences who aren't in SF yet
Manually Added A team member creates a lead directly in Salesforce Business card scans, referral introductions, trade show contacts

Lead Status Values

Every Lead has a Status field that tracks where it is in the review process.

StatusMeaningWhat's Next
New Just arrived in Salesforce — not yet reviewed by anyone Daily script will attempt auto-conversion. If no match, stays as New.
Working Someone is actively reviewing or attempting to identify/contact this lead Update notes, try to identify company, prep for conversion
Qualified Lead has been reviewed and is a legitimate prospect worth pursuing Conversion to Contact is imminent; or auto-conversion handles it
Unqualified Lead is not a fit for MMA — spam, irrelevant industry, student, etc. Mark as Unqualified and leave — do not convert
Converted Lead has been successfully converted to a Contact (and linked to an Account) Conversion complete. The lead record is archived — the person now lives as a Contact.

The Daily Auto-Conversion Process

MMA runs a Python script called lead_convert_prep.py every morning. This script is the right way to handle lead conversion at scale. Here's how it works:

1
Script runs each morning

The automation queries all leads with Status = New or Working and reviews them for conversion eligibility.

2
Company matching

The script compares the lead's Company Name and Email Domain against existing Salesforce Accounts. It looks for exact or close matches.

3
Match found → Convert

If a matching Account is found, the lead is automatically converted to a Contact on that Account. All fields are mapped. Status becomes Converted.

4
No match found → Lead stays

If no matching Account is found, the lead remains unconverted. It stays in the leads queue until a match can be made manually or the company is added to SF.

5
Persistent non-matches → Holding accounts

Leads that can't be matched over time may be placed on the UNKNWN or Admin holding account for manual review and cleanup.

⚠️
Why the automation matters: The auto-conversion script handles field mapping, duplicate detection, and campaign member updating all at once. Manual conversions bypass these safeguards and can create inconsistent or duplicate data. Trust the script.

What You Can Do With Leads

Even though you shouldn't convert leads manually, there are things you CAN do:

  • View leads — Open the Leads tab to see incoming inquiries. You can read their details, form responses, and UTM data.
  • Update the Status — If you've reviewed a lead and confirmed it's spam or unqualified, you can set Status to Unqualified.
  • Add notes — Use the Notes & Attachments or Activity sections to log context about a lead.
  • Report on leads — Run reports on leads by source, form type, or date to understand where inquiries are coming from.
  • Flag for manual review — If a lead is from a high-value company and hasn't auto-converted, flag it for Aaron Sultan to review.
💡
Spotting a high-value lead: Check the lead's Company Name and Title. If the company is a well-known brand and the title is VP or above — and the lead hasn't been converted in a few days — it's worth flagging for follow-up.
