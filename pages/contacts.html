Contacts (People)

A Contact is a specific person at a company. Understanding how contacts are structured — including membership inheritance, mailable criteria, and board flags — is essential for all outreach and reporting work.

What Is a Contact?

In Salesforce, a Contact is an individual person — an executive, marketer, account manager, or any employee at a member or non-member company. Every Contact must be linked to an Account (their company). Contacts without an Account cannot exist in the standard Salesforce data model.

Contacts are the primary audience for MMA's email outreach, event invitations, and membership communications. Understanding which contacts are "mailable" is one of the most common tasks in Salesforce.

💡
Remember: A contact's membership status comes from their Account. If you want to know if someone is a member, look at Account.Status__c — or at the Contact's Account_Status__c field, which mirrors it.

What Makes a Contact "Mailable"?

When building lists for email campaigns or event invitations, a contact must meet all of the following criteria to be included. This is often called the "mailable" definition at MMA.

Mailable Criteria Checklist

  • ✅
    Active Account: Their Account's Status__c must start with "Active" (Active-Parent, Active-Subsidiary, Active-Local Council, etc.)
  • ✅
    NA or Global Region: Their Account's Region__c must be NA or Global (for US/Global outreach — regional teams have their own lists)
  • ✅
    Has an Email Address: The Email field must not be blank
  • ✅
    Not Opted Out: HasOptedOutOfEmail must be false
  • ✅
    Not Bounced: IsEmailBounced must be false
  • ✅
    Not a Board Member: Is_Board_Member__c = false AND Current_B2B_CMO_Council_member__c = false (board members get separate, custom outreach)
⚠️
All six conditions must be true simultaneously. A contact who has an active account but has opted out of email should never receive mass outreach. Missing any one of these filters can result in emailing people who shouldn't receive the message.

How to Search for a Contact

  1. Use the global search bar at the top of Salesforce. Type the person's name or email address.
  2. Salesforce will show matches across all objects — click the result under "Contacts."
  3. If searching by email domain (e.g., all contacts at Nike), go to Contacts list views and filter by Email contains 'nike.com'.
  4. For large searches, use Reports — navigate to Reports, create or open a "Contacts" report, and apply filters.
💡
Searching by company: The fastest way to find all contacts at a company is to open the Account record, then scroll to the "Contacts" related list at the bottom of the page. This shows every contact linked to that account.

What to Do If a Contact Is on the Wrong Account

Sometimes a contact ends up on the wrong Account — for example, they appear under "UNKNWN" or an old company they no longer work for. Here's how to handle it:

Steps to Reassign a Contact

  1. Open the Contact record.
  2. Click Edit (or the edit pencil on the Account Name field).
  3. In the "Account Name" field, search for and select the correct company Account.
  4. Save the record. The contact will now appear under the correct account and inherit that account's membership status.
  5. Verify that the contact's Account_Status__c and Is_Member__c fields updated correctly after saving.
🚫
Do not move contacts off the UNKNWN account without checking with Aaron Sultan first. The UNKNWN account is part of the lead conversion pipeline. Moving contacts out of it manually can interfere with automated cleanup scripts.

Newsletter Engagement — RASA & DATT

MMA uses two newsletter/content delivery systems — RASA and DATT — that sync engagement data back to Salesforce. These fields track email opens, clicks, content preferences, and engagement scores at the contact level. They are supplemental engagement signals, not substitutes for the mailable definition.

⚠️
These fields are still being refined. RASA and DATT engagement data is actively integrated and evolving. Use them as directional signals for engagement — do not use them as hard filters for campaign suppression without checking with Aaron Sultan.

Creating a New Contact

If you need to add someone to Salesforce who doesn't exist yet:

  1. First search for them — they may already exist as a Lead or under a different account.
  2. Navigate to the Contacts tab and click New.
  3. Fill in: First Name, Last Name, Email, Title, and — critically — the Account Name (their company).
  4. If their company doesn't exist as an Account yet, you'll need to create the Account first, then create the Contact linked to it.
  5. Do NOT create a contact without linking it to an Account.
⚠️
Check for duplicates before creating. Salesforce may warn you about potential duplicate contacts. Always review the duplicate alert — if the person already exists, edit their existing record rather than creating a second one.

Contact Type

The Contact Type field (Contact_Type_1__c) marks contacts who hold a named formal role at their member account — the billing rep, the main membership contact, a board director, a council member. These are the people Shauna and the membership team specifically place and maintain. Never bulk-move, merge, or auto-process contacts where this field is set.

🚫
Contact Type = protected record. Any contact with Contact_Type_1__c populated must be excluded from bulk automation, data migrations, list imports, and cleanup scripts. These contacts have intentional placements. Combined with Is_Board_Member__c, they form MMA's "do not auto-touch" set.

Contact Type vs. Is_Board_Member__c

⚠️
