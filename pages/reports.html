Reports vs. Dashboards — What's the Difference

Salesforce has two distinct tools for viewing data. They're related but serve different purposes:

📊 Dashboards
Visual summaries — charts, graphs, and metric tiles built on top of reports. Great for a quick at-a-glance read on how something is trending. Dashboards refresh on demand but don't show individual records. You go to a dashboard to understand the big picture.
Example: How many members attended events this year? What's our YTD engagement breakdown?
📋 Reports
Tabular data — actual rows of records with filters applied. Reports are how you get a real list: every attendee, every CMO contact, every board member. Exportable to Excel. Reports power dashboards, but they're also used directly as working lists.
Example: Who attended the last CCS? Which Marketer members don't have a CMO key role set?
💡
When to use which: Open a dashboard when you want a status check. Open a report when you need a list — to export, to send an email to, to hand off to someone, or to QA data. Most day-to-day work starts with a report.

Tips for Working with Reports

🔍
Finding a report: Go to the Reports tab in Salesforce → search by name. If you're not sure of the exact name, filter by folder. Most key reports live in Public Reports, AS Events Dashboard, or 2026 Key Audience Lists.
📥
Exporting: From any report, click the dropdown arrow next to "Edit" → "Export" → choose Excel format. Always check the row count after export — Salesforce truncates exports over 2,000 rows unless you use the full export option.
⚠️
Don't run reports directly as email lists: Reports in Salesforce don't apply Pardot-specific suppression (hard bounces, global suppressions, Pardot do-not-email). Always run audience pulls through the proper mailable filters or use an approved audience from the 2026 Key Audience Lists folder before sending through Pardot.
🗂️
Need a new report? Submit a request via the Salesforce request form referenced in the governance policy. Aaron Sultan can also build or modify reports — use the request process to ensure it gets tracked and built correctly.
