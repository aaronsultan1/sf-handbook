Training Pathway

A self-serve curriculum for MMA staff. Work through the levels that match your role — each one builds on the last and links directly to the relevant handbook sections.

Everyone with a Salesforce login should complete Level 1. From there, continue to the levels that match how you actually use the system. You don't need to do all four — go as deep as your role requires.

💡
How to use this pathway: Read through each section in the handbook, then use the checklist to confirm you can do each task on your own. If you get stuck or something isn't covered, submit a request or reach out to Aaron.

1
🌱 Foundation
Required for everyone   Anyone with a Salesforce login, regardless of how often you use it.
After this level you'll be able to: Understand how MMA uses Salesforce, know the rules around data and access, find contacts and accounts, and know how to ask for help the right way.
📖 What to read
  • 🛡️
    Governance & Ownership
    Full page — especially Data Ownership, How Contacts Enter SF, and the Request Process
  • 👤
    Contacts — What Is a Contact?
    Focus on: Key Fields, What Makes a Contact Mailable, and Board Member Flags
  • 🏢
    Accounts — What Is an Account?
    Focus on: Marketer vs Solution Provider definitions and the quick test
  • ⚡
    Quick Reference
    Bookmark this — useful for day-to-day lookups
✅ Skills checklist
  • I know the difference between a Contact and an Account in MMA's system
  • I understand why some contacts can't receive emails (mailable criteria)
  • I know the 5 ways contacts enter Salesforce
  • I understand who owns what data (the RACI model)
  • I know how to submit a Salesforce request using the correct form
  • I know not to create duplicate contacts or accounts without checking first
  • I can look up a contact and understand their key fields
🎓 Trailhead — complete these modules
  • Salesforce Platform Basics Module
  • Who Sees What in Salesforce Module
  • Data Management Module
2
🔧 Standard User
Recommended for regular users   Anyone who actively works in Salesforce — managing contacts, tracking events, running reports.
After this level you'll be able to: Work independently in Salesforce on day-to-day tasks — creating and updating contacts, managing campaign membership, building and running reports, and tracking opportunities.
📖 What to read
  • 👤
    Contacts — full page
    Creating contacts, reassigning, engagement tracking, event tags
  • 🏢
    Accounts — full page
    Membership logic, ecosystem values, account types
  • 📣
    Campaigns
    How campaigns work, campaign members, statuses
  • 📊
    Reports & Dashboards
    Building reports, standard filters, saving to folders
  • 🎯
    How-To Guides
    Step-by-step walkthroughs for common tasks
✅ Skills checklist
  • I can create a new contact and fill in all required fields correctly
  • I can reassign a contact to the correct account
  • I can add a contact to a campaign and set the correct status
  • I understand what Opportunity records are and when to create them
  • I can build a basic report filtered by region, account type, or campaign
  • I know where to save reports so the right people can find them
  • I understand what engagement tracking fields mean on a contact record
  • I can identify when a contact record has a data quality issue
🎓 Trailhead — complete these modules
  • Accounts & Contacts for Lightning Experience Module

  • Reports & Dashboards for Lightning Experience Trail
  • Opportunity Management Module
3
📧 Pardot / Email User
Marketing & comms users   Anyone who sends emails, builds lists, or runs email campaigns through Pardot / Account Engagement.
After this level you'll be able to: Build compliant email lists, understand how Salesforce and Pardot stay in sync, segment contacts beyond basic lists, send list emails, and know the rules around what you can and can't do.
📖 What to read
  • 📧
    What Is Pardot?

  • 📧
    Lists
    Static vs dynamic lists, how to build each, naming conventions
  • 📧
    List Scoring, Rule Groups & Segmentation Rules
    Multi-condition logic, segmentation rules, dynamic filtering
  • 📧
    Automation Rules & Engagement Studio
    When to use each — ongoing automation vs. one-off sends
  • 📧
    Sending a List Email
    Step-by-step send process and pre-send checklist
  • 📧
    Compliance
    Required reading — GDPR, opt-outs, what you can't do
✅ Skills checklist
  • I understand how contacts sync between Salesforce and Pardot
  • I know what makes a contact "mailable" before adding them to a list
  • I can create a static list and a dynamic list
  • I can build a dynamic list with multiple conditions using rule groups
  • I know when to use a segmentation rule vs. an automation rule
  • I understand when to use Engagement Studio vs. a one-off list email
  • I can send a list email and connect it to a Salesforce campaign
  • I follow MMA's naming conventions for lists and emails
  • I understand opt-out fields and would never manually override them
  • I know what to do if an email hard-bounces
🎓 Trailhead — complete these modules
  • Pardot Lightning App Basics Module
  • Pardot Business Units Module
  • Account Engagement Specialist Prep Cert Trail
  • Pardot Segmentation Module
4
⚙️ Power User / Regional Admin
Regional leads & admins   Team members who manage regional data quality, coordinate with the global team, or work with integrations.
After this level you'll be able to: Manage data quality for your region, understand how MMA's integrations work, identify and escalate data issues, and handle advanced scenarios without needing to escalate everything to Aaron.

    Admin Training Requirements
    Ongoing habits and Trailhead recommendations for admins
✅ Skills checklist
  • I understand how Apollo, Rasa, and ZoomInfo feed into Salesforce
  • I can identify duplicate contacts and know the correct process to resolve them
  • I know which fields my region is responsible for keeping current
  • I can run a data quality report for my region's contacts
  • I understand the difference between Work Tier 1, 2, and 3 requests and when to escalate
  • I know what changes require Aaron's approval vs. what I can do myself
  • I can train a new team member using this handbook independently
