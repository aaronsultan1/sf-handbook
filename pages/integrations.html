Integrations — Detailed Reference

📧
Pardot / Account Engagement
Salesforce's marketing automation & email platform — MMA's primary outreach tool
Active — Core Bidirectional Sync Native SF Integration

Pardot (officially "Marketing Cloud Account Engagement") is MMA's email marketing platform. It is deeply integrated with Salesforce — every mailable Contact in SF has a corresponding Prospect in Pardot. The two systems sync automatically: changes to key fields in one propagate to the other.

What Pardot does for MMA: Sends all mass email campaigns (event invitations, newsletters, member communications), tracks email opens and clicks, manages mailing lists and suppression lists, and records engagement back into Salesforce.

How the sync works: A Pardot connector runs continuously. When a Contact is created or updated in Salesforce, it syncs to Pardot as a Prospect (if it meets the mailable criteria). When a Prospect interacts with an email in Pardot (open, click, form fill), that engagement data writes back to Salesforce fields prefixed pi__.

Regional note: EMEA and APAC teams use separate Pardot Business Units (different BU_ID) but share the same Salesforce org. Bülent Doruker manages the EMEA Pardot setup and regional email sends.

Key SF Fields (prefix: pi__)

pi__pardot_hard_bounced__c pi__score__c pi__grade__c pi__last_activity__c pi__campaign__c
📤
Outreach
Sales sequencing & outreach automation — used for targeted membership outreach campaigns
Active One-Way Push to SF

Outreach is a sales engagement platform used primarily by the US/Global membership team for multi-step outreach sequences — series of emails and tasks to prospect or re-engage member contacts. Outreach sequences are built in the Outreach platform and the contacts (called "Prospects" in Outreach) are synced from Salesforce.

How Salesforce is involved: When a contact is added to an Outreach sequence, Outreach writes the sequence ID and status back to Salesforce via the Outreach_Id__c field on the Contact record. This allows the SF team to see which contacts are in active outreach sequences.

Pardot integration: Outreach sequences can feed into Pardot suppression lists. The Python script outreach_seq_to_pardot_list.py pulls all prospects from a given Outreach sequence and adds them to a Pardot list (e.g., suppression for a specific campaign). This is run on demand by Aaron Sultan.

Who uses it: Primarily the US membership sales and renewal team. Outreach access is separate from Salesforce access — contact Aaron Sultan or your team lead to be set up.

Key SF Fields

Outreach_Id__c

Rasa.io
AI-personalized newsletter delivery — tracks newsletter engagement in Salesforce
Active Writes to SF Fields

Rasa.io is MMA's newsletter platform, which uses AI to personalize content for each subscriber. MMA sends regular newsletters through Rasa.io to member contacts, and Rasa.io writes engagement data (opens, clicks, subscription status) back to Salesforce fields prefixed rasa_.

What the fields capture: Whether a contact is subscribed to the MMA newsletter, their engagement level with recent issues, and their subscription preferences. These fields are useful for segmenting highly engaged contacts versus those who may need re-engagement outreach.

Key SF Fields (prefix: rasa_)

rasa_subscribed__c rasa_unsubscribed__c rasa_last_opened__c rasa_engagement__c

MediaRadar
Ad spend intelligence — API connector that surfaces media buying activity for member & prospect companies
Active — API Connector Writes to SF Fields

MediaRadar is an advertising intelligence platform that tracks media spend for brands and agencies. MMA connects to MediaRadar via API, and spend data is pushed directly into Salesforce Account records — giving the membership team ad investment context without ever leaving SF.

Practical use: When preparing for a membership renewal or prospecting call, checking the MediaRadar fields on the Account gives you a sense of the company's media investment level. Higher spend typically correlates with stronger interest in MMA's research and advocacy work.

Who manages it: Primarily used by the membership sales team. Contact Aaron Sultan if you have questions about the data or notice it's out of date.

Key SF Fields (prefix: MediaRadar_)

MediaRadar_Total_Spend__c MediaRadar_Digital_Spend__c MediaRadar_Last_Updated__c
💼
LinkedIn Sales Navigator
Native API connector — surfaces LinkedIn profile data and enables InMail from within Salesforce
Active — API Connector Native SF Package

MMA connects to LinkedIn Sales Navigator via a native Salesforce API integration (namespace LID__). The connector syncs LinkedIn profile data directly to Contact records and enables the membership sales team to send LinkedIn InMails and view Sales Navigator insights without leaving Salesforce.

What it provides: LinkedIn profile links, company matching signals, and InMail access from the Contact record. The LID__ fields are populated on contacts that have been matched to a LinkedIn profile through the connector.

Who uses it: Primarily the membership sales team for prospecting and outreach via LinkedIn Sales Navigator.

Key SF Fields (prefix: LID__)

LID__LinkedIn_Member_Token__c LID__LinkedIn_Company_Id__c
🔎
Apollo
Manual enrichment — used to fill in names & titles for contacts missing data
Script-Based No Native SF Package

Apollo.io is a B2B sales intelligence platform used by the US/Global team for on-demand enrichment. Apollo does not have a native Salesforce package — it is accessed via API through Python scripts maintained by Aaron Sultan.

How it's used: When a batch of contacts is missing names or job titles (e.g., after importing from an event registration list), Aaron runs an enrichment script that queries Apollo's API with the contact's email address, gets back name/title/company data, and writes it back to Salesforce directly via the Salesforce API.

What it can find: First name, last name, job title, company name, LinkedIn URL. Apollo matches by email address — if it can't find the email, the contact is skipped (not created in Apollo).

Transparency: There are no Apollo-specific fields in Salesforce. Enriched data goes directly into the standard FirstName, LastName, Title fields. You won't be able to tell post-enrichment whether a field came from Apollo or a human.

Web-to-Lead Forms
Native Salesforce feature — how MMAGlobal.com forms flow into SF
Active — Core Native Salesforce

Web-to-Lead is a native Salesforce feature that processes form submissions from any web page and creates Lead records in Salesforce. Every form on MMAGlobal.com — event registration, content download, member inquiry, survey — is a Web-to-Lead form.

The critical link — every form = a Campaign: When a Web-to-Lead form is set up, it is linked to a specific Salesforce Campaign record. The form includes a hidden field containing the Campaign ID. When someone submits the form, the resulting Lead is automatically added as a Campaign Member on that Campaign. This is how MMA tracks which events/webinars people registered for, which content they downloaded, and which forms they've filled out.

Campaign Member Status on form submission: The default status for new Campaign Members from forms is "Responded." This updates to "Attended" or similar statuses later if the person attends the event.

Field: Web_To_Lead_Forms__c on the Contact record is a list/text field that summarizes which forms the contact has previously submitted. It's a quick-reference field for account managers reviewing a contact's touchpoint history.

⚠️
Do NOT manually convert Leads from Web-to-Lead submissions. The nightly automated conversion script handles this. Manually converting a Lead created by a Web-to-Lead form can break campaign membership tracking and create duplicate contacts.

Engagement Tracking Fields

Multiple integrations contribute to a set of Contact-level engagement tracking fields that roll up a person's activity across all platforms. These are useful for segmentation and identifying highly engaged vs. disengaged contacts.

FieldWhat It CapturesSource
Last_event_participation__c Date of the most recent in-person event or webinar attendance CampaignMember Status updates
Last_webinar_participation__c Date of the most recent webinar registration or attendance CampaignMember Status updates
Last_engagement_any__c Date of most recent engagement of any type (event, webinar, survey, etc.) Rollup from CampaignMember + Additional_Touchpoint__c
Engagement_level__c Composite engagement tier (e.g., Highly Engaged, Engaged, Inactive) Calculated based on engagement recency and frequency
Is_Disengaged__c Boolean — true if no engagement in the past 12 months Calculated field / Flow update
pi__score__c Pardot engagement score — based on email opens, clicks, form fills Pardot sync
