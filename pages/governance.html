How Contacts Enter Salesforce

Contacts don't just appear — they come in through several defined pathways. Understanding these pathways helps you know whether to look for someone as a Contact, a Lead, or somewhere in between.

The Five Entry Points

1. Web Forms → Leads → Auto-Conversion

Every registration form, download form, and sign-up page on MMAGlobal.com is connected to Salesforce as a Web-to-Lead form. When someone submits a form, a Lead record is automatically created. A nightly automation script then runs and:

  1. Looks up the email against existing Contacts
  2. If a match is found, updates the existing Contact (no duplicate created)
  3. If no match is found, converts the Lead into a new Contact under the appropriate Account
  4. Logs the form submission as a Campaign Member on the campaign linked to that form
⚠️
Do NOT manually convert Leads. The automation script handles this nightly. Manual conversion can create duplicates, miscredit campaigns, or break the pipeline. If a Lead seems stuck, contact Aaron Sultan.

2. Pardot / Account Engagement Sync

When a contact is created in Salesforce, it automatically syncs to Pardot (MMA's email marketing platform). Likewise, when a prospect fills out a Pardot-tracked form, a new Contact (or Lead) can appear in Salesforce. The sync is bidirectional — changes in one system propagate to the other for mailable contacts. See the Integrations page for full details.

3. Manual Entry by Staff

Any MMA employee with edit access can manually create a Contact. This is typically done when: a new member joins and the primary contact needs to be added immediately; someone at a member company is referred and not yet in the system; a regional team adds local contacts during in-person events. Always search before creating — duplicates are the #1 data quality problem in SF.

4. Enrichment Tools (ZoomInfo, Apollo)

ZoomInfo automatically enriches existing Contact and Account records with updated titles, phone numbers, and company information via a native Salesforce integration (fields prefixed DOZISF__). Apollo is used by the US/Global team for manual enrichment passes on contacts missing names or titles — it does not have a native Salesforce package; updates are applied via Python scripts.

5. Event/Campaign Registration

When someone registers for an MMA event or webinar, they're added to the corresponding Campaign as a Campaign Member. If they don't already exist as a Contact, a Lead is created (and later converted). Event registrations are the single largest source of new contacts entering the system each year.

Web Forms & Campaign Connections

Every public-facing form at MMA — registration pages, download gates, survey links, event sign-ups — is built on Salesforce's Web-to-Lead framework and linked to a Campaign record. This is how MMA tracks who engaged with what, when.

How It Works

  • A new form is created in Salesforce as a Web-to-Lead form. The form is linked to a specific Campaign record.
  • The form embeds a hidden field containing the Campaign ID.
  • When someone submits the form, Salesforce creates a Lead record and also adds them as a Campaign Member on that campaign.
  • The nightly lead conversion script picks up the Lead and converts it to a Contact (or updates the existing Contact).
  • The Campaign Member record persists — this is how event attendance and registrations get tracked in SF reports.
💡
Field: Web_To_Lead_Forms__c on the Contact record shows which forms the contact has previously submitted. This provides a quick summary of their form-based touchpoints with MMA.
⚠️
Pardot Forms vs. Web-to-Lead Forms: Regional teams (especially EMEA) may use Pardot forms directly rather than Web-to-Lead forms. Pardot forms create Pardot Prospects (not Leads) and sync to Salesforce contacts via the Pardot connector. The Campaign connection still exists — it's just routed differently. Both paths end at the same Contact record.

Data Quality Standards

Salesforce is only as useful as the quality of the data inside it. These are MMA's current data quality rules — all staff are expected to follow them when creating or editing records.

Contact Quality Rules

RuleWhy It Matters
Every contact must be linked to an Account Orphaned contacts can't inherit membership status and won't appear in member mailing lists
Email address is required for any new contact Without an email, the contact can never be reached or tracked in Pardot
No duplicate contacts Duplicates inflate mailing lists, skew engagement reporting, and create confusion in account management
First Name + Last Name must be real names Generic placeholders like "Zoom Room" or "Test User" pollute reports. Flag Need_company_researched__c or Need_email_researched__c if info is incomplete.
Job title should be present for named contacts Title is used for segmentation (e.g., CMO audiences, Executive programs)
Contacts should not be left on the UNKNWN account UNKNWN is a holding pen for unmatched leads. Aaron Sultan runs cleanup scripts to move them — don't intervene manually without checking first.

Helpful Data Quality Fields

FieldWhat It FlagsAction
Need_email_researched__c Contact is missing or has a bad email address Research correct email via ZoomInfo, LinkedIn, or Apollo
Need_company_researched__c Contact name or company is unknown/placeholder Identify the person's real name and company, update the record
Data_Quality_Score__c Composite score reflecting overall record completeness Higher is better. Low-score contacts are candidates for enrichment
MismatchUSA__c / MismatchEMEA__c / MismatchAPAC__c / MismatchLATAM__c Contact appears to belong to a different region than their current Account Review the contact's account assignment and move if needed

Requesting Salesforce Access

New MMA employees are set up with Salesforce access as part of IT onboarding. If you need access or need your permissions adjusted:

  • New user setup — Contact IT/your manager. IT creates the Salesforce user account and assigns the appropriate profile.
  • Read-only access — Standard for most non-membership staff. Can view all records but cannot edit.
  • Edit access — For Membership team members and regional CRM leads. Required to create/edit contacts, campaigns, and opportunities.
  • Admin access — Restricted to IT and Aaron Sultan (US/Global). Required to modify field definitions, workflows, and system settings.
  • Questions about permissions? — Ask Aaron Sultan (US/Global data) or your regional CRM lead (APAC: Rohit Dadwal; EMEA: Bülent Doruker; LATAM: Fabiano).
🚫
Do not share Salesforce login credentials. Every user must have their own account. Sharing logins makes audit trails meaningless and violates MMA's data security policy. If you need elevated access temporarily, ask for your own profile to be updated.

Creating a Contact — Step by Step

Follow these steps any time you need to manually add a new person to Salesforce. Most contacts arrive automatically via web forms and lead conversion — manual creation should be the exception, not the rule.

  • 1
    Search first Use the global search bar at the top of Salesforce. Search by full name AND by email address. Also check the Leads tab — the person may already exist as a Lead awaiting overnight conversion. Creating a duplicate is worse than waiting.
  • 2
    Confirm the Account exists Before creating the Contact, find the company's Account in Salesforce. If the company doesn't exist yet, you'll need to create the Account first (fill in Account Name, Account Type, Status, Region, and Primary Ecosystem — see the Accounts page).
  • 3
    Navigate to Contacts → New Click the Contacts tab in the top navigation, then click the New button in the top right. Alternatively, open the Account record and click New Contact from the Contacts related list — this pre-fills the Account field for you.
  • 4
    Fill in required fields Minimum required: First Name, Last Name, Email, Account Name. Also fill in: Title (job title), Mobile/Phone if known. Do not use placeholder names like "Unknown" or "Test."
  • 5
    Review the duplicate alert Salesforce will warn you if it finds similar records. Read the alert — if a matching contact exists under a different Account, edit that existing record instead of creating a new one. If the alert shows a genuinely different person, proceed with the new contact.
  • 6
    Save and verify membership inheritance After saving, scroll down on the Contact record and verify that Account_Status__c and Is_Member__c have populated correctly from the linked Account. If they're blank, the Account may not have its Status field set.
  • 7
    Notify the relevant team if needed If you added a contact for a member company in a region you don't own (e.g., you're US team and added someone at a Turkish member), give the regional owner (Bülent for EMEA) a heads-up so they can assign the contact to their queue and set any regional fields.

Requesting Salesforce Work

All Salesforce work requests — from minor field updates to major process redesigns — must go through the proper channel. This keeps requests tracked, prioritized, and delivered with the appropriate level of quality control.

📬
Submit all requests by email: Send to aaron@mmaglobal.com and CC membership@mmaglobal.com. Once received, Global Support reviews the request, assigns a work tier, and shares an estimated timeline.

To avoid delays, include the following in every request:

  • Clear objective — What are you trying to accomplish?
  • Specific requirements — What exactly needs to be built or changed?
  • Business context — Why is this needed and what process does it support?
  • Desired timeline — Is there a hard deadline or event driving this?
  • Impacted teams or regions — Who else will be affected by the change?

Work Tiers & SLAs

Every request is classified into one of four tiers based on complexity and impact. Tiers determine the SLA and the level of review required before changes go live.
