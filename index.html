What is Salesforce at MMA?

Salesforce is MMA's central CRM (Customer Relationship Management) system. It stores every company (Account), every person (Contact), every inbound inquiry (Lead), every event and webinar (Campaign), and every deal or membership renewal (Opportunity).

Think of Salesforce as the single source of truth for who MMA's members are, what events people attended, when memberships renew, and who the right person to contact is at any given company. If it matters to MMA's business, it lives in Salesforce.

MMA has regional teams across US/Global, APAC, EMEA, LATAM, and MEA — each with slightly different workflows. This handbook covers both the shared foundations and the regional differences.

Ground Rules

🚫
Do not manually convert Leads. MMA runs a daily automated script that converts leads to contacts. If you manually convert a lead, you may create duplicates or break the automation. Coordinate with Aaron Sultan if you have questions about a specific lead.
🚫
Do not edit the UNKNWN account. This is a system placeholder account for unmatched leads. Contacts here are in a queue for cleanup. Do not move contacts to or from it manually.
🚫
Do not add contacts to "Holding for Non-Accounts." This is a legacy account that predates MMA having a Salesforce admin. It was used as a workaround when someone changed jobs but their new company didn't exist in Salesforce yet. That workaround is now obsolete — always create the real Account instead. If you see contacts sitting here, flag them to Aaron Sultan for proper re-assignment.
⚠️
"Holding Pattern" contacts are being tracked intentionally — do not delete them. When a known contact leaves their company and doesn't yet have a new position, they're moved to the Holding Pattern account. MMA keeps tabs on senior executives in transition so they can be re-engaged when they land somewhere new. Do not remove or archive these contacts without checking with Aaron Sultan first.
⚠️
Membership lives on the Account, not the Contact. If someone's membership status looks wrong, always check their Account record first. The Contact simply inherits the Account's status.
💡
Campaign naming convention matters. MMA's scripts that sync data to Pardot, Outreach, and reporting rely on consistent campaign names. Always follow the REGION_TYPECODE_YYYYMMDD_EventName format. See the Campaigns page for full details.

How Do I...

🔍

Find if a company is a member

  • 1Search for the company name in the Salesforce global search bar
  • 2Open the Account record
  • 3Look at the Status__c field — any value starting with "Active" = member
  • 4Check Next_Renewal_Date__c to see when they renew
👤

Find all contacts at a company

  • 1Search for the company name in global search
  • 2Open the Account record
  • 3Scroll down to the "Contacts" related list
  • 4All contacts at that company are listed there
📣

Create a new campaign for an event

  • 1Go to Campaigns tab → click "New"
  • 2Name: follow REGION_TYPECODE_YYYYMMDD_EventName
  • 3Set Type (Webinar, Forum/Event, etc.) and Status (Planning or Active)
  • 4Fill in Start/End Date, Region__c, Event_Name__c
  • 5If it's a session under a larger event, set the ParentId
  • 6Save — the campaign is ready for members to be added
➕

Add someone to a campaign

  • 1Open the Campaign record
  • 2Scroll to "Campaign Members" related list
  • 3Click "Add Members" and search by name or email
  • 4Set Status: Registered (pre-event) or Attended (post-event)
  • 5Click Add / Save
🔄

Move a contact to a different account

  • 1Open the Contact record
  • 2Click Edit (or the pencil icon next to Account Name)
  • 3Search for and select the correct Account
  • 4Save — the contact now inherits the new account's membership status
  • 5Verify Account_Status__c and Is_Member__c updated correctly
💼

Create an Opportunity for a renewal

  • 1Navigate to the Account record for the member company
  • 2Scroll to "Opportunities" related list → click "New Opportunity"
  • 3Name: "2026 [Region] Membership Renewal_CompanyName"
  • 4Type: Membership - Renewal; Stage: Prospecting
  • 5Set Close Date (expected renewal date) and Amount
  • 6Advance stages as the deal progresses
🏗️

Build a mailable contact list (US/Global)

  • 1Go to Reports → Create a Contacts report
  • 2Filter: Account.Status__c LIKE 'Active%'
  • 3Filter: Account.Region__c IN ('NA', 'Global')
  • 4Filter: HasOptedOutOfEmail = false
  • 5Filter: IsEmailBounced = false
  • 6Filter: Email != null
  • 7Filter: Is_Board_Member__c = false AND Current_B2B_CMO_Council_member__c = false
🆕

Add a new contact to Salesforce

  • 1First, search for them — they may already exist
  • 2Go to Contacts tab → click "New"
  • 3Fill in: First Name, Last Name, Email, Title
  • 4Account Name: link to their company Account (required)
  • 5If their company isn't in SF, create the Account first
  • 6Review the duplicate warning before saving

Accounts (Companies)

An Account represents a company. Every Contact, Opportunity, and Campaign interaction eventually ties back to an Account. Understanding Account structure is foundational to using Salesforce at MMA.

What Is an Account?

In Salesforce, an Account is a company — a brand, agency, tech platform, media company, or any organization that has a relationship with MMA. Every person (Contact) must belong to an Account. Every membership renewal (Opportunity) is tied to an Account. If you want to know whether a company is a member, you look at their Account record.

MMA has thousands of Accounts ranging from active global member corporations to non-member companies that have simply attended an event or expressed interest in the past.

⚠️
Membership lives on the Account, not the Contact. When you want to know if someone is a member, look at their Account's Status__c field — not the Contact directly. The Contact's membership-related fields are just mirrors of the Account.

Membership Status

The most important field on an Account is Status__c. This is the primary membership filter used across all MMA reports, scripts, and data exports.

💡
The golden rule: To find all members, filter Status__c LIKE 'Active%'. Any Status__c value beginning with "Active" means the company is a current member.

Status__c Values Explained

Active-Parent
The main (parent) membership. This company holds the primary membership record. Most global and national members fall here.
Active-Subsidiary
A subsidiary or child company covered under a parent company's membership. They get member benefits through the parent.
Active-Subsidiary (irrelevant)
Covered under a parent, but classified as "irrelevant" — typically too small or inactive to be meaningfully engaged. Still technically active.
Active-Local Council
Member of a regional Local Council (common in APAC). These accounts are active but tracked separately from global memberships.
Pending
Membership is in process — a deal is likely in the Opportunity pipeline, but not yet closed/paid.
Inactive/Cancelled
Was once a member but their membership has lapsed or was cancelled. Important to track for win-back efforts.
Non-Member
Has never been a member, or the account was created for tracking purposes only. No active membership relationship.

Account Hierarchy: Parent & Child Accounts

Large companies often have multiple Salesforce records — a parent company and one or more subsidiary accounts. This is called an Account Hierarchy.

Example: A Large Holding Company

Imagine a major holding company like a global advertising group. In Salesforce it might look like:

  • Parent Account: "GlobalAdGroup Inc." — Status: Active-Parent — holds the membership
  • Child Account A: "GlobalAdGroup Agency NYC" — Status: Active-Subsidiary — covered under parent
  • Child Account B: "GlobalAdGroup Media Buying" — Status: Active-Subsidiary (irrelevant) — covered but not engaged

Contacts at any of these three accounts have member access, but the primary membership relationship is managed at the parent level.

💡
Tip: When looking at a contact's membership, always check if their account is a subsidiary. If so, navigate up to the parent account to see the full membership details, renewal date, and dues.

Ecosystem (Industry Type)

The Primary_Ecosystem__c field on Account classifies what type of company a member is within the marketing and media industry. It drives segmentation for outreach, reporting, and membership composition analysis. As of 2026, MMA has 1,016 active member accounts across all ecosystems.

🧭
The most important segmentation in Salesforce: Every active member account is either a Marketer or a Solution Provider. That two-way split is the foundation for almost all audience segmentation at MMA — used in reports, mailable counts, outreach campaigns, and board/council targeting. Everything else flows from understanding this distinction.

The Core Divide: Marketer vs. Solution Provider

A company whose marketing team is an internal function — they spend money on advertising and media to promote their own brand, products, or services to consumers. They are the ones buying the marketing ecosystem's outputs.

Ecosystem Values — Definitions & Examples

Marketer
Brands and advertisers. Their marketing team is internal — they buy media and run campaigns to sell their own products or services. Spans retail, CPG, financial services, healthcare, auto, hospitality, and more. The largest single ecosystem in MMA's membership.
Agency
Companies that manage marketing on behalf of brands. Includes media agencies (GroupM, Publicis, IPG Mediabrands, Omnicom), creative agencies, PR firms (Weber Shandwick), research/measurement firms, and strategy consultancies that run marketing practices. They plan, buy, and execute on behalf of clients.
Seller
Companies that own and sell media inventory or ad space. Includes social/digital platforms (Google, Meta, TikTok, LinkedIn, Pinterest, Snap, Reddit), streaming services (Netflix, Hulu, Spotify, Disney+), traditional broadcasters (ESPN, NBCUniversal), and retail media networks (Amazon Ads, Walmart Connect, CVS Media Exchange). They monetize audiences.
Enabler
Technology and data companies that power the marketing infrastructure. Includes ad tech platforms (The Trade Desk, AppsFlyer), measurement & verification (DoubleVerify, Integral Ad Science, Nielsen), identity/data (LiveRamp, TransUnion, Amperity), and marketing software (Adobe). They build the tools marketers and sellers use.
Carrier/Operator
Telecom network operators whose infrastructure underpins mobile marketing. Currently one active member globally: MTN (EMEA). Historically more relevant when SMS and carrier-based mobile was a primary channel.
Associate
Non-commercial affiliated members — research institutes, trade associations, academic-adjacent organizations. Examples: The Advertising Research Foundation (ARF), Marketing Science Institute, Analytic Partners. Not typical commercial members but formally affiliated with MMA.
Academic Institution
Universities and research institutions with an academic membership type. Rare in active count — most appear in specific regional markets.
Holding (for holding accounts only)
Reserved exclusively for Holding Pattern and similar internal-use accounts. Should never appear on a real company record. If you see this on a normal Account, flag it.
Start Up
Early-stage companies in the marketing/media/technology space, typically in a startup membership tier. Rare in the current active member count.

Edge Cases & Nuances

The ecosystem field was assigned manually by people who understood each company's primary role. A few patterns worth knowing:

Same company, different ecosystem depending on the entity

Walmart (the retailer) = Marketer — they advertise to consumers to drive store traffic. Walmart Connect = Seller — they sell ad inventory to brands on the Walmart platform. Two separate Account records, two different ecosystems, both correct.

Google (global) = Seller. Google India Private Limited = Enabler. Different legal entities, different primary functions in that market.

AT&T and Verizon = Marketer — even though they're telecoms, they're members because of their marketing function (spending billions on advertising), not their carrier function.

Kantar appears as both Agency and Enabler

Different Kantar entities do different things. Kantar Argentina, Kantar Vietnam = Agency (they provide research services to brands). Kantar (global), Kantar Germany = Enabler (they provide measurement and data products). When you see Kantar, check the specific Account record.

How to Pull CMOs for a Member Segment (Python — 2-step pattern)

SOQL can't filter Contact mailable criteria through a Key Role relationship traversal in a single query. Use this two-step approach:

# Step 1 — get active Marketer accounts with CMO populated
accounts = sf.query("""
    SELECT Id, Name, CMO__c, CMO__r.FirstName, CMO__r.LastName, CMO__r.Email
    FROM Account
    WHERE Status__c LIKE 'Active%'
      AND Region__c IN ('NA', 'Global')
      AND Primary_Ecosystem__c = 'Marketer'
      AND CMO__c != null
""")

# Step 2 — apply mailable + board exclusion to those Contact IDs
cmo_ids = [a['CMO__c'] for a in accounts['records']]
ids_str = "'" + "','".join(cmo_ids) + "'"
contacts = sf.query(f"""
    SELECT Id, FirstName, LastName, Email, Title, Account.Name
    FROM Contact
    WHERE Id IN ({ids_str})
      AND Email != null
      AND HasOptedOutOfEmail = false
      AND IsEmailBounced = false
      AND Is_Board_Member__c = false
      AND Current_B2B_CMO_Council_member__c = false
""")
⚠️
Board members can hold Key Roles. A contact listed as the CMO in CMO__c may also be a board member. For invite-based programs (e.g., CCS) where board members should be excluded, apply the standard board exclusion in Step 2. For all-CMO mailings where board members are OK to include, omit those filters — your choice depends on the use case.

When building lists for email campaigns or event invitations, a contact must meet all of the following criteria to be included. This is often called the "mailable" definition at MMA.

What Is a Lead?

Think of leads as the "waiting room" of Salesforce. When someone fills out a form on MMAGlobal.com, registers for a webinar, or is imported from an event list, they typically start as a Lead. The Daily Auto-Conversion Process

MMA runs a Python script called lead_convert_prep.py every morning. This script is the right way to handle lead conversion at scale. Here's how it works:

1
Script runs each morning

The automation queries all leads with Status = New or Working and reviews them for conversion eligibility.

2
Company matching

The script compares the lead's Company Name and Email Domain against existing Salesforce Accounts. It looks for exact or close matches.

3
Match found → Convert

If a matching Account is found, the lead is automatically converted to a Contact on that Account. All fields are mapped. Status becomes Converted.

4
No match found → Lead stays

If no matching Account is found, the lead remains unconverted. It stays in the leads queue until a match can be made manually or the company is added to SF.

5
Persistent non-matches → Holding accounts

Leads that can't be matched over time may be placed on the UNKNWN or Admin holding account for manual review and cleanup.

⚠️
Why the automation matters: The auto-conversion script handles field mapping, duplicate detection, and campaign member updating all at once. Manual conversions bypass these safeguards and can create inconsistent or duplicate data. Trust the script.

What You Can Do With Leads

  • View leads — Open the Leads tab to see incoming inquiries. You can read their details, form responses, and UTM data.
  • Update the Status — If you've reviewed a lead and confirmed it's spam or unqualified, you can set Status to Unqualified.
  • Add notes — Use the Notes & Attachments or Activity sections to log context about a lead.
  • Report on leads — Run reports on leads by source, form type, or date to understand where inquiries are coming from.
  • Flag for manual review — If a lead is from a high-value company and hasn't auto-converted, flag it for Aaron Sultan to review.
💡
Spotting a high-value lead: Check the lead's Company Name and Title. If the company is a well-known brand and the title is VP or above — and the lead hasn't been converted in a few days — it's worth flagging for follow-up.

How Contacts Enter Salesforce

Contacts don't just appear — they come in through several defined pathways. Understanding these pathways helps you know whether to look for someone as a Contact, a Lead, or somewhere in between.

The Five Entry Points

1. Web Forms → Leads → Auto-Conversion

Every registration form, download form, and sign-up page on MMAGlobal.com is connected to Salesforce as a Web-to-Lead form. When someone submits a form, a Lead record is automatically created. A nightly automation script then runs and:

  1. Looks up the email against existing Contacts
  2. If a match is found, updates the existing Contact (no duplicate created)
  3. If no match is found, converts the Lead into a new Contact under the appropriate Account
  4. Logs the form submission as a Campaign Member on the campaign linked to that form
⚠️
Do NOT manually convert Leads. The automation script handles this nightly. Manual conversion can create duplicates, miscredit campaigns, or break the pipeline. If a Lead seems stuck, contact Aaron Sultan.

2. Pardot / Account Engagement Sync

When a contact is created in Salesforce, it automatically syncs to Pardot (MMA's email marketing platform). Likewise, when a prospect fills out a Pardot-tracked form, a new Contact (or Lead) can appear in Salesforce. The sync is bidirectional — changes in one system propagate to the other for mailable contacts. See the Integrations page for full details.

3. Manual Entry by Staff

Any MMA employee with edit access can manually create a Contact. This is typically done when: a new member joins and the primary contact needs to be added immediately; someone at a member company is referred and not yet in the system; a regional team adds local contacts during in-person events. Always search before creating — duplicates are the #1 data quality problem in SF.

4. Enrichment Tools (ZoomInfo, Apollo)

ZoomInfo automatically enriches existing Contact and Account records with updated titles, phone numbers, and company information via a native Salesforce integration (fields prefixed DOZISF__). Apollo is used by the US/Global team for manual enrichment passes on contacts missing names or titles — it does not have a native Salesforce package; updates are applied via Python scripts.

5. Event/Campaign Registration

When someone registers for an MMA event or webinar, they're added to the corresponding Campaign as a Campaign Member. If they don't already exist as a Contact, a Lead is created (and later converted). Event registrations are the single largest source of new contacts entering the system each year.

Web Forms & Campaign Connections

Every public-facing form at MMA — registration pages, download gates, survey links, event sign-ups — is built on Salesforce's Web-to-Lead framework and linked to a Campaign record. This is how MMA tracks who engaged with what, when.

How It Works

  • A new form is created in Salesforce as a Web-to-Lead form. The form is linked to a specific Campaign record.
  • The form embeds a hidden field containing the Campaign ID.
  • When someone submits the form, Salesforce creates a Lead record and also adds them as a Campaign Member on that campaign.
  • The nightly lead conversion script picks up the Lead and converts it to a Contact (or updates the existing Contact).
  • The Campaign Member record persists — this is how event attendance and registrations get tracked in SF reports.
💡
Field: Web_To_Lead_Forms__c on the Contact record shows which forms the contact has previously submitted. This provides a quick summary of their form-based touchpoints with MMA.
⚠️
Pardot Forms vs. Web-to-Lead Forms: Regional teams (especially EMEA) may use Pardot forms directly rather than Web-to-Lead forms. Pardot forms create Pardot Prospects (not Leads) and sync to Salesforce contacts via the Pardot connector. The Campaign connection still exists — it's just routed differently. Both paths end at the same Contact record.

What Is Pardot?

Pardot — officially rebranded as Salesforce Marketing Cloud Account Engagement — is MMA's primary email marketing platform. Every mass email sent to members (event invitations, newsletters, webinar promotions, research alerts) goes through Pardot.

Pardot works with Salesforce contacts through a concept called Prospects. Every mailable contact in Salesforce has a corresponding Prospect record in Pardot. The two systems sync automatically and continuously.

Salesforce ↔ Pardot Sync

Understanding the sync is critical. Changes in one system propagate to the other — but there are rules about which system "wins" when there's a conflict.

How the Sync Works

  • Every Salesforce Contact with an email address can sync to a Pardot Prospect. Contacts missing an email address are not synced.
  • The sync runs continuously (near real-time). New Contacts created in Salesforce appear in Pardot within minutes.
  • Salesforce is the system of record for identity data (name, email, company, title). Pardot wins on engagement data (score, grade, opens, clicks).
  • If HasOptedOutOfEmail = true in Salesforce, Pardot will also suppress that prospect. The opt-out syncs both directions.
  • If a Pardot email hard bounces, Pardot sets IsEmailBounced = true in Salesforce automatically.

Sending a List Email

A List Email is a one-time send to one or more lists — the most common send type at MMA for event invitations, webinar promotions, and member announcements.

Step-by-Step: Send a List Email

  1. Go to Emails > List Emails > "+ Add List Email"
  2. Name the email — use a clear internal name: CCS 2026 — Round 1 Invite — 2026-04-15
  3. Select your template — choose the appropriate pre-built template
  4. Fill in email details — Subject line, From name, From email (must use MMA domain), preheader text
  5. Add sending list(s) — Pardot deduplicates if a prospect is on multiple lists
  6. Add suppression list(s) — critical. Add lists for already-registered contacts, opted-out sequences, exclusions

  8. Schedule or send immediately — once sent, the email cannot be recalled. Double-check everything first.

Email Best Practices

Apply these best practices to every MMA email campaign, regardless of audience size.

Subject Lines

  • 50–65 characters is the sweet spot — displays fully in most desktop and mobile clients without truncation
  • Front-load the key message — the most important word should come first; mobile clients truncate early
  • Personalize when possible — adding the recipient's name or company can lift open rates
  • Avoid spam-trigger words — FREE, URGENT, CLICK NOW, excessive ALL CAPS, multiple exclamation points (!!!)
  • Test before committing — test one variable at a time (emoji vs. no emoji, question vs. statement)

Pre-Header Text

The pre-header is the preview snippet that appears after the subject line in most inboxes. It should complement the subject — not repeat it. Keep it under 100 characters. If left blank, email clients pull the first visible line of the email body (often the unsubscribe link or image alt tag).

Content Length

Email TypeTarget Word CountWhy
Event invite / cold outreach100–150 wordsGet to the point. Busy executives won't scroll through a wall of text.
Newsletter / member update250–300 wordsSubscribers expect more content — keep it scannable with headers and bullets.
Reminder / follow-up50–100 wordsShorter is better for reminders — just the CTA and key details.

Responsive Design & CTAs

  • Single-column layout — multi-column side-by-side designs break on mobile
  • Minimum 14px font size for body text — smaller is hard to read without zooming on a phone
  • CTA buttons at least 44px tall — thumb-friendly on touchscreens
  • Images under 200kb — always include alt text; never send an email that's only images (spam filters block it)
  • One primary CTA above the fold + one secondary CTA in the body
  • Action-oriented CTA text: "Register Now", "View the Report", "Save Your Seat" — not "Click Here"

Segmentation & Personalization

  • Segment before you send — a Marketer member and a Solution Provider should receive tailored messaging, not the same generic blast
  • Use variable tags: {{Recipient.FirstName}}, {{Recipient.Company}}, etc.
  • Test merge fields before send — send a test to an account with known values to confirm tags populate correctly
  • Set fallback values — if a contact has no first name, "Hi there" is better than a blank space

Send Timing

  • Best days: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
  • Best time: 9–11am Eastern
  • Avoid: Mondays (post-weekend inbox overload), Fridays, major US holidays
  • For global sends: Use Eastern as baseline; EMEA and APAC sends go through their respective BUs with local timing
